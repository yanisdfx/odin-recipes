Quiche Lorraine

Description

The quiche Lorraine is a traditional French savory tart that originates from the Lorraine region in northeastern France. It features a buttery, flaky pastry crust filled with a rich mixture of eggs, cream, and smoked bacon (lardons).

Simple yet flavorful, the quiche Lorraine is often enjoyed warm or at room temperature, served as a main dish with a salad or as part of a brunch. Its creamy texture and balanced taste make it one of the most iconic and beloved dishes in French cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 shortcrust pastry (or pie crust)
  • Smoked Bacon
  • Heavy cream
  • 3 eggs
  • Emmental cheese
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pastry and place it in a tart pan. Prick the bottom with a fork and pre-bake it for about 10 minutes (this prevents it from getting soggy).
  3. Cook the bacon in a pan until golden and slightly crispy. Drain the excess fat.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, cream, and milk. Add salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  5. Spread the bacon evenly over the pastry crust. If you’re using cheese, sprinkle it on top.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the filling.
  7. Bake for about 30–35 minutes, or until the top is golden and the filling is set.
  8. Let it cool slightly before serving.
