Crème brûlée

Description

Crème Brûlée is a classic French dessert featuring a rich custard base topped with a layer of caramelized sugar.

Smooth, creamy, and slightly sweet, it’s often flavored with vanilla and served chilled, offering a perfect contrast between the silky custard and the crunchy caramel top.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (480 ml) heavy cream
  • 1 vanilla bean (or 1 teaspoon vanilla extract)
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar (plus extra for caramelizing)
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. Heat the cream with the split vanilla bean (or vanilla extract) until warm, but not boiling. Remove from heat and let it infuse for a few minutes.
  3. Whisk the egg yolks with sugar and a pinch of salt until smooth and pale.
  4. Slowly pour the warm cream into the egg mixture while stirring continuously.
  5. Strain the mixture to remove any lumps or vanilla bean pieces.
  6. Pour into ramekins and place them in a baking dish. Fill the dish with hot water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins (water bath).
  7. Bake for 35–40 minutes, until the custard is set but still slightly wobbly in the center.
  8. Cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  9. Just before serving, sprinkle sugar on top and caramelize with a kitchen torch or under a broiler until golden and crispy.
